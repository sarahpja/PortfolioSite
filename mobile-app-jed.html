Mobile App Design -JED

Problem

Many shoppers who want to support small business find it hard to trust sellers and unsure how much of their money is going back to the sellers instead of the company who owns the platform.

Solution

JED, a platform that focuses on building trust between the seller and customers. Customers have access to detailed information on the product and a cost break down of each item they purchased. Information about the sellers are easy to find and customers can also support them by donations.

Timeline

3 Weeks

What I Did

Identify user problems brainstorm for solution, and create prototype for testing

  • User Researcher
  • Competitor analysis
  • Ideation
  • Prototyping

Research & Analysis

We conducted five interviews to learn about users' online shopping habits and the pain points of buying things online, especially from small businesses.Using an affinity diagram, we grouped similar ideas together to further define a user persona.

Brainstorm & Ideation

We identified customer goals, the challenges they face, the root cause of their problems, and summarized in a problem statement. We explored possible solutions using the "I like", "I wish", "what if" framework, then selected the features we want to keep with the feature prioritization matrix.

We created an user scenario and storyboard that outline their journey as they explore the app for the first time.

Wireframing & Prototyping

A user flow was created from the user journey map. Each group member created paper sketches. Dot voting and user test results were used to select the desired features to include in the digital prototype.

User Testing & Outcome

Users test was conducted with multiple paper prototypes . We asked our users to complete three separate tasks. The results showed that users feel more confident with prototypes that clearly displayed the price and breakdown of cost.

Conclusion & Future Opportunities

The most challenging part of this project was keeping the style consistent across the different screens. Without a style guide, we had to reorganize and reassign responsibilities by reducing the number of people working on the prototype. If we were to continue with this project, the next step would be meeting with different teams to gather inputs to further refine the design.
