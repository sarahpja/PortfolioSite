Redesign - Department of Education

Problem

The current website of the U.S Department of Education does not have a useful navigational menu and certain landing pages are not accessible except through the FAQs section. People have difficulties finding the information they need. The mobile version of the homepage is not responsive, many links can't be selected without zooming in.

Solution

A redesign of the navigation menu, desktop and mobile homepage.

Timeline

3 Weeks

What I did

Analyze and conduct research with existing design as a team. Redesign, test the prototype individually, then iterate base on user feedback.

  • Heuristic Evaluation
  • Prototyping
  • User Testing

After

Research & Analysis

We started the research with heuristic evaluation of the homepage. It scored a 1.2 out of 3 for aesthetics, 2.3 for content, 1.8 for navigation, and 1.9 for functionality. We conducted usability test with the existing homepage and found that users had trouble completing the tasks when the information they need is not listed on the homepage or the L1 navigation.

Brainstorm & Ideation

To gather ideas on topics and links to include in the navigation menu, I looked at the FAQ's, content in the L1 landing pages, and the operating structure of the department. I used card sorting to regroup based on categories and hierarchy.

Wireframing & Prototyping

I started with wireframing the desktop homepage. I added a hero for the most important message and cards for featured news. I went with a simple design and removed side navigation menus to de-clutter the page. For mobile displays, the navigation was rolled up into the hamburger menu.

User Testing & Outcome

Users were asked to perform four tasks involving finding information on the mobile website. A total of three users participated. Changes were made to improve the prototype after each user's test outcome. The last user had the easiest time completing the tasks. All users agree that the new website is easier to navigate and scan.

Conclusion & Future Opportunities

This project showed the importance of information architecture. It was difficult to decide how to reorganize the topics. If I were to continue this project in real life, it would be helpful to conduct A/B test with live traffic to see if people can find the information they need more efficiently.
