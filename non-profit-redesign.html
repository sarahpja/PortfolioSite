Redesign - Alley Cat Allies

Problem

User is interested in finding information that will help them get started with helping out cats in their community. They navigated to the “Take Action” page on Alley Cat Allies, but have problem finding relevant information that meet their needs.

Solution

We believe that redesigning the navigation and “Take Action” page for cat lovers who would like to help out their community cats will help them find the information they need faster and arrive at specific landing pages consistently without getting lost.

Timeline

2.5 Weeks

What I did

Redesign the "Take Action" landing page following the design process.

  • User Research and Interview
  • Style Guide
  • Prototyping
  • User Testing

After

Research & Analysis

We began by defining and understanding our users. We created a proto-persona named Serena whose goal is to find information on the site to help her with the cats in her local community. Then we evaluated the heuristics of the navigation and "Take Action" landing page. Competitor analysis was conducted against several similar non-profit organizations to gain insight into each one's strengths and weaknesses. To gather more detailed feedback on the usability of the existing website, we set up a survey and interviewed cat lovers and prompted them to interact with the website as our proto-persona.

Brainstorm & Ideation

We gathered ideas and inspiration with moodboards, sketched out new layouts for the landing page, and reorganized navigation menu items by card sorting. A style guide was created for consistency across different parts of the new design.

Wireframing & Prototyping

We created wireframes for the landing page and navigation menu. The new landing page would have a hero image with a clear CTA that direct users to local resources with the input of a zip code. We moved actionable items towards the top of the page and created unique sections for campaigns, events, and newsletter sign-up. The navigation menu was reorganized into four categories and minimize into a hamburger menu on mobile.

To test the useability of the new design, we asked our users to rate the landing page from 1 to 5 and compare the existing and new navigation menu. We took the feedback from users and further improved the design a couple more times. Graphic only logo was replaced with the logo with text on mobile. Instead of overlaying the CTA text, it was moved below the image to make it easier to read. A description was added to the event below the event image.

Conclusion & Future Opportunities

Both the users and designers find it difficult to stay within the limited scope of this project. As designers, we had to imagine content on other pages that are not part of the redesign and have a vision of what they might look like. Our users had to imagine that they will see what they expect after clicking on a link. Many of the current problems exist because users found that the description or name of a link does not match its content. Our prototype has only one page, therefore we could not show how a complete solution would look. Further work will likely include inputs from people working at the organization and reorganization of the site's information architecture.
